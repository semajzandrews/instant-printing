# Instant Printing — SIGNATURE

## Concept: "Into Register"
A print shop's one true moment is the instant misaligned color separations snap
into register and ink becomes a finished sheet. The site stages that as a
dreamlike, depth-layered descent: floating proofs, paper, and ink plates drift in
parallax, and at the heart of the page three offset CMY-toward-violet separation
plates converge into one crisp printed sheet as you scroll.

## Lane (locked)
- Accent: `#5B3FA0` (press-ink violet) as the single ink, on a soft warm paper ground.
- Display font: **Author** (Fontshare), humanist editorial sans.
- Layout: layered parallax depth (foreground proofs / mid sheet / back plates).
- Mood: dreamlike — soft light, floating layers, slow drift, gentle grain.

## Signature moment (ONE)
**The Registration** — a scroll-coupled stack of three separation plates
(cyan-ish, magenta-ish, and the violet key) that begin offset and drift into
perfect register as the "Pressroom" section enters, resolving into a single
sharp printed sheet. Scroll-coupled transform only (no paint thrash), degrades to
the registered sheet under prefers-reduced-motion.

Distinct from past signatures (hover-reveal cards, scroll-braid, breathing ring,
house-elevation draw, barber-pole spine, EKG line, swatch wall, thermostat dial):
this is a depth-layered color-separation convergence, new mechanic + new mood.

## Why it beats the cliché
Print-shop sites default to CMYK-rainbow logos, clip-art printers/ink drops, and
"FAST CHEAP" banner soup. This is a single-ink violet, paper-grounded, editorial,
dreamlike depth piece. A visitor has not seen a corner print shop look like this.
